PRESS/DAILY HAMPSHIRE GAZETTE


Animator puts device into motion
by LARRY PARNASS

At one point in his career, Northampton artist Luke Jaeger labored to create countless drawings to feed animation's insatiable appetite for the images it needs to put, ever so briefly, before a viewer's eyes.

For a new project, Jaeger went cosmic - cinematically speaking. He created an enormous coin-operated movie machine that's now on view at Lowell's experimental Revolving Museum. Jaeger says his steel sculpture, "Wayback," is based on the functioning of the zoetrope, the 19th century forerunner to motion pictures. By inserting 50 cents, as if using a jukebox, people can order up a flickering image. Jaeger created the piece while enrolled in a graduate program at the Massachusetts College of Art in Boston, then trucked it home. "It was in my garage for six months. Thank God it's gone," he said of "Wayback." The piece is part of "Play-Land," an exhibit that includes a host of sculptural games and interactive works. The museum, founded in 1984, is known for its experimentalism - and its humor. "We're almost an anti-museum, from what people are saying," said its founder and artistic director, Jerry Beck. In its literature, the museum notes: "Don't get us wrong, we appreciate the Picassos of the world, but we get more excited about the potent creativity in everyday life." Beck said Jaeger's "Wayback" conjures up images of an old mechanical carousel. "You're watching the image dance before your eyes," he said. "It's huge and it's heavy. It has got that industrial, mad scientist, engineer thing." For years, Beck has cultivated a roster of artists willing to jump boundaries. Jaeger, he suggested, is a natural at it. At Mass. Art, Jaeger decided to learn how to weld, which enabled him to build "Wayback." "It's like a frontiersman trying to find new territory," Beck said of Jaeger. Recently, Jaeger has been teaching art classes in a variety of schools. His goal now, with degree in hand? "I hope to get one stable job that's close to home," he said.
