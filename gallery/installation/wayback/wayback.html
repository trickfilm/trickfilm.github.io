WAYBACK, my MFA thesis exhibit, is a coin-operated zoetrope (a 19th-century animation machine). The moving wheel is wood and glass, mounted in an industrial-grade steel frame.

Gallery visitors are invited to deposit coins in the wall-mounted coin box, which resembles a contemporary payphone. The coin activates the motor, lights, and music for about a minute. While the zoetrope is spinning, animated drawings mounted on glass inside the spinning drum appear to dance.

It's big, heavy, and loud. The 150-pound turntable is driven at 70 rpm by a beefy washing machine motor. The steel structure stands 10 feet high and occupies a 5-foot square footprint on the floor. I've never weighed it but I estimate the whole thing weighs about 1500 pounds.
