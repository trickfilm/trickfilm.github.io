CELIA JOHNSON
(3 minutes, HD digital)
Public domain and original imagery combine in this lush Winterpills music video, bursting with an abundance of exotic plants, extinct fauna, and antique illustrations.
     
  FISHWIFE
(4 minutes, HD digital)
An enigmatic dancing man and a dog-headed woman celebrate the birth of a fish-child, then watch as it takes flight. This bittersweet animated short evokes parenthood’s complex emotional landscape.
     
  DYING STAR
(4 minutes, HD digital)
A subterranean landscape of found and archival footage forms a lush backdrop for the sad and mysterious Winterpills song of the same name.
     
  CAROLINA SHOUT
(4 minutes, HD digital)
The latest in a series of “antique music videos” is set to a 1921 composition by jazz pioneer James P. Johnson, an originator of the Harlem Stride piano style. The animation reflects the multi-leveled, over-the-top delight and frantic silliness of this amazing musical performance. In bringing these old musical treasures to new audiences I hope to recreate in some small way the experience of hearing this music for the first time.
     
  WHEN ME BODY LAY DOWN IN THE GRAVE
(4 minutes, HD digital)
The third in a series of “antique music videos” is a requiem for the numberless laborers who died building our physical and cultural environment. A haunting, minor-key shout recorded in 1938 accompanies the imagery of hard labor and death in the subterranean spaces and skyscraper steelwork of New York.

  OLD JOE’S HITTIN’ THE JUG
(4 minutes, 16mm, black & white)
Music by 1930s hot jazz fiddler Stuff Smith accompanies a mix of live action, stop-motion and puppetry, including a 15-foot-high plywood marionette suspended from a highway overpass. Shot entirely outdoors in various weed-choked, industrial-looking locations in Northampton, Massachusetts, the film draws a parallel between these neglected, formerly useful sites which have gone to seed, and the musical treasures which the ever-changing machineries of cultural production have left in obscurity. Behind-the-scenes type pictures here!
     
  OUT THE FIRE
(4 minutes, 16mm, color) An “antique music video” homage to the animation of the Fleischer Studios, set to a 1946 calypso about the New York City Fire Department. The film functions as a fun cartoon but also mourns the disappeared past of New York and asserts the power of music as a mechanism for retrieving the unretrievable. Cel and paper animation.
     
  5 DREAMS
(7 minutes, 16mm, color)
A rapid-fire collage of dream-inspired animation, the film highlights animation’s historical-kinship with the dream world, a protean realm where anything can turn into anything. “A fireworks of visual imagination and surreal abstraction” — Victoria (BC) Independent Film & Video Festival. Mixed 2D and 3D techniques, including drawing, collage, xerography, stop-motion, and digital animation. Soundtrack by Birdsongs Of The Mesozoic.
     
  BIG CONCRETE PLACE
(10 minutes, 16mm, color)
Orange-headed cartoon protagonist in a fruitless search for a screw removed from his head by a malevolent fly. Dark and sursreal in the manner of Svankmajer and Road Runner cartoons, the film conjures a world in which characters’ fates are controlled by capricious objects (or animators). Full cel animation.
